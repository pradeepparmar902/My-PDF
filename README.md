📖 How to use PDF Workbench
📁1. Adding your PDF files
At the very top of the page is a box that says "Drop PDF files here, or click to browse."
1. Either drag a PDF file from your computer straight onto that box, or click the box and choose a file the
normal way.
2. You can select more than one file at a time — every page from every file you add lands together in the
work area below, ready to be arranged.
3. To add more files later, use the "+ Add PDFs" button in the toolbar (the row of buttons just under the
dropzone).
Nothing you upload leaves your computer — all the work happens right inside your browser.
🔗2. Merging multiple PDFs into one
You don't need a separate "merge" button — merging happens automatically. As soon as you add more
than one PDF, all their pages sit together in the same work area, in the order they appear on screen.
1. Add your first PDF, then add your second (and third, and so on) using "+ Add PDFs."
2. Arrange the pages into the order you want them in the final file (see "Reordering pages" below).
3. Click the green "⬇ Download merged PDF" button at the top right of the toolbar. This downloads one
single PDF file containing every page, in the order shown on screen.
↕3. Reordering pages
Each page appears as its own little card with a number badge in the corner showing its position.
1. Click and hold on any page card, drag it to where you want it, and let go — the page moves there and
every page number updates automatically.
2. If you have a lot of pages and need to drag one a long way, drag your mouse near the very top or
bottom edge of the screen — it will scroll automatically while you're still holding the page.
3. You can also reorder using the small list on the right-hand side of the screen (the Page navigator) the
same way — drag a row up or down.

🗑4. Deleting pages
There are two ways to remove pages you don't want:
1. One page at a time: click the small

 in the corner of any page card to remove just that page.
2. Several pages at once: click on a page (or tick its checkbox) to mark it — marked pages get a red
outline and a "Delete" stamp. Mark as many as you like, then click "Delete selected" in the toolbar.
3. Use "Select all" or "Clear selection" in the toolbar to quickly mark or unmark every page at once.
Made a mistake? Use the "↺ Undo last change" button in the toolbar straight after — it reverses your most
recent action (one step only).
📋5. The "Files in this project" list
On the right-hand side, above the page list, you'll see every PDF you've uploaded, numbered 1, 2, 3 and
so on, with how many of its pages are currently in your workbench.
1. If you realize you uploaded the wrong file, click the 
✕
 next to that file's name.
2. You'll be asked to confirm — once you do, only that file's pages are removed. Every other file and its
pages stay exactly as they were.
3. Then just use "+ Add PDFs" to upload the correct file instead.
🧭6. Page navigator & full-size preview
When you have a lot of pages, scrolling through a big grid to find one page can be slow. The panel on the
right-hand side solves that.
1. It lists every page as a small thumbnail with its page number and file name.
2. Click any row to open that page in a large preview at the top of the screen, so you can see it clearly
without zooming.
3. Each row also has its own checkbox so you can select pages (for deleting or splitting) without having
to scroll through the whole page grid.
4. You can also double-click any page thumbnail in the main grid to open the same large preview.
✂7. Splitting out specific pages into their own PDF
Use this when you only want to save or share a few pages, rather than the whole document.
1. Tick the pages you want (using the checkboxes on the page cards or in the navigator on the right).
2. Click the gold "✂ Split selected" button in the toolbar.
3. A window opens showing just your chosen pages — you can untick any you change your mind about.
4. Choose whether to also remove these pages from the main workbench after exporting (off by
default, so splitting doesn't disturb your main file unless you ask it to).
5. Click "✂ Export split PDF" to download just those pages as their own separate PDF file.

🔍8. Finding and replacing text
This works on real, selectable text in the PDF (the kind you could normally click and copy) — not text
that's part of a picture or logo (for that, see the next section).
1. Scroll down to the "Find & replace text" section.
2. Type the word or phrase you want to find, and what you want to replace it with.
3. Click "Scan document". Every match found across all your pages will be listed, showing the original
text crossed out and the replacement next to it.
4. Untick any matches you don't actually want changed.
5. The changes are only applied when you click "Download merged PDF" — nothing is altered until then.
This works best on clean PDFs with plain text and a white background. It won't find words inside a scanned
page or a picture/logo.
✎ 9. Changing text that's part of a logo, picture, or scanned page
If a word you need to change is actually part of an image — like a company name baked into a logo —
the Find & Replace tool above can't reach it, because there's no real selectable text there, just a picture.
This tool covers that case by hand.
1. On any page card, click the small pencil (
✎
) button.
2. A larger view of that page opens. Click and drag a box over the exact spot you want to change (for
example, directly over a company name in a logo).
3. A small form appears — type the new text, and adjust the colors if needed (the cover color is
automatically guessed from the page behind it, so it usually doesn't need changing).
4. Click Save. You'll see a small purple badge appear on that page's card showing it now has a patch.
5. If the same logo appears on every page (like a letterhead), tick the box to apply this patch to all
pages instead of repeating the steps for each one.
6. You can drag a patch to move it, drag its corner handles to resize it, or click it again to edit or delete it
— at any time before downloading.
7. Click "Done" to close the editor. As with everything else, the patch is only baked into the file once you
click "Download merged PDF".

📝10. Turning a PDF into an editable Word document
Use this when you want to open and edit the content in Microsoft Word, rather than just reading it as a
PDF.
1. Scroll to the "Extract text to a Word document" section.
2. Choose whether to convert all pages, or only the ones you've ticked.
3. Leave "Use OCR for scanned/image pages" ticked if some of your pages are scanned photos rather
than typed text — this lets the tool "read" the words off the picture and turn them into real, editable
text.
4. Click "📝 Extract & download Word file". A progress list shows what's happening page by page
(scanned pages take longer than typed ones).
5. A .docx file downloads automatically once it's done, ready to open in Word.
This rebuilds the words and basic formatting (bold, headings, and side-by-side columns where they're
detected) — it does not produce a pixel-for-pixel copy of the original PDF's exact design.
📊11. Turning a PDF into an Excel spreadsheet
Use this for PDFs that contain tables of figures — like a financial statement, schedule, or list — that you'd
like to work with in Excel.
1. Scroll to the "Extract text to an Excel spreadsheet" section.
2. Choose which pages to include, the same way as the Word tool.
3. Choose your Sheet layout: either a separate tab for every PDF page, or everything combined into one
single tab.
4. Click "📊 Extract & download Excel file". Pages that look like real tables get laid out into proper rows
and columns automatically; pages that don't will still come through as plain text, one line per row.
Very tightly packed or unusually shaped tables might not line up perfectly — a quick check and tidy-up in
Excel afterward is always a good idea for anything important.

Make sure you've already downloaded anything you want to keep before clicking "New project" — it cannot
be undone.

💡13. A few general tips
Nothing is saved or changed in your final PDF until you actually click a download button — feel free to
experiment freely beforehand.
Working with a very large PDF (many pages, or large scanned images) may take a little longer to load
and process — this is normal.
If you're trying the scanned-page (OCR) features and they don't seem to start, try downloading this
tool and opening it directly in your browser rather than viewing it inside another app's preview
window — some preview windows restrict background processing that OCR needs.
You can come back to this guide any time by clicking "📖 How to use this" at the top of the page.
