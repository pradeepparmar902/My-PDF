Find & replace text

Scans every page's real text for a match and swaps it during export. Best on clean, text-based PDFs with white backgrounds — scanned/image pages have no searchable text, and the replacement won't perfectly match the original font. For text baked into a logo or scanned image (like a name inside an image), use the ✎ pencil button on that page's card instead — it lets you paint over the exact spot by hand.

OCR language (for scanned or handwritten pages)

Tell the OCR engine which language(s) might appear on a scanned page before extracting to Word or Excel — it's used by both tools below. Reading printed/typed text is reasonably reliable. Handwriting is much harder: neat, clearly separated lettering reads best, while cursive or naturally varied handwriting — especially in Hindi or Gujarati script, where there's far less public training data — will often come back partial or rough. Treat OCR'd handwriting as a rough first draft to proofread, not a final transcript.

Extract text to a Word document

Pulls real text out of normal PDF pages — keeping paragraphs, bold, rough heading sizes, and side-by-side columns where it detects them — and runs OCR automatically on any page that's actually a scanned image with no real text underneath, so even scanned PDFs convert. Everything happens in your browser; large or scanned documents take longer because OCR runs page by page. Column detection is best-effort and works well for clean two-column layouts (like address blocks or newsletter-style body text); very complex or irregular layouts may still need manual cleanup. Note: if you're trying this inside a chat preview window, OCR may be blocked by that preview's security sandbox — download the file and open it directly in a browser tab for OCR to work. Normal text extraction works fine either way.

Extract text to an Excel spreadsheet

Looks for table-like rows of data — figures lined up in columns, like financial statements or schedules — and lays them out as real spreadsheet columns. Pages without a clear table structure still export, just as one row of text per PDF line. OCR runs automatically on scanned/image pages, same as the Word export. Column detection is best-effort: very clean tables come out cleanly, dense or irregular ones may need a little tidying afterward.

Tip: text replacements and image patches are both only baked in the moment you click "Download merged PDF" — nothing is written until then. Reordering and deleting pages happen instantly, but you get one step of undo if you change your mind.
